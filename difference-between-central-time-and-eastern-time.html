What is Central Time?

Central Time is the time zone used in the Central Time Zone of the United States and Canada. It is one hour behind the Eastern Time Zone and six hours behind the Coordinated Universal Time (UTC). It is also referred to as America/Chicago and Central Standard Time (CST). It covers the states of Arkansas, Illinois, Iowa, Louisiana, Minnesota, Mississippi, Missouri, North Dakota, South Dakota, Wisconsin, and parts of Kansas, Nebraska, Oklahoma, and Texas.

What is Eastern Time?

Eastern Time is the time zone used in the Eastern Time Zone of the United States and Canada. It is five hours behind the Coordinated Universal Time (UTC). It is also referred to as America/New York and Eastern Standard Time (EST). It covers the states of Connecticut, Delaware, District of Columbia, Florida, Georgia, Maine, Maryland, Massachusetts, New Hampshire, New Jersey, New York, North Carolina, Ohio, Pennsylvania, Rhode Island, South Carolina, Vermont, Virginia, and parts of West Virginia and Ontario, Canada.

The Time Difference Between Central and Eastern Time

The time difference between Central Time and Eastern Time is one hour. Central Time is one hour behind Eastern Time. For example, if it is 12:00 PM Eastern Time, then it is 11:00 AM Central Time.
