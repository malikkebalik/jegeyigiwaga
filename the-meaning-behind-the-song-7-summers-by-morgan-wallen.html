The History of 7 Summers

Morgan Wallen’s hit song “7 Summers” was released in August 2020 and quickly landed in the top 10 on the Billboard Country Music chart. But the history of the song goes back to the early days of Wallen’s career – before he was a household name.

Wallen co-wrote “7 Summers” with Josh Osborne and Shane McAnally back in 2018 while on tour with his band. However, it wasn’t until a year later that the trio revisited the song and re-worked it into what it is today.

The Story Behind the Song

At face value, “7 Summers” seems like a simple love song, describing a summer fling that didn’t last. But as Wallen explains in interviews, there’s much more to the story.

The song’s lyrics describe a summer romance that fizzles out when the girl goes back to college in the fall. But it’s not just about losing a love interest – it’s about missing out on life experiences and feeling stuck in place.

In the second verse, Wallen sings: “But I look back on those neon lights, and all those good old times, and we’d go drinking on the riverbank, til the morning light.”

These lines paint a picture of carefree youth and the excitement of discovering new experiences. It’s a theme that resonates with many listeners, regardless of their age or background.

The Music Video

The music video for “7 Summers” further cements the song’s themes of nostalgia and longing. Shot in black and white, the video features Wallen driving around a small town, reminiscing on his past and yearning for a lost love.

Many of the shots in the video are framed through a car window or shot from the passenger seat, adding to the feeling of being stuck in one place. It’s a poignant representation of the song’s themes and a visual feast for fans.

The Impact of the Song

Since its release, “7 Summers” has become an anthem for anyone feeling stuck in place or missing out on life experiences. It’s a relatable and timeless theme, and the song has resonated with fans of all ages and backgrounds.

The song’s success cemented Wallen’s status as one of the hottest up-and-coming stars in country music. It spent eight weeks in the top 10 of the Billboard Country Music chart and has garnered over 181 million streams on Spotify alone.

The Personal Connection

For Wallen, “7 Summers” is more than just another hit song. In interviews, he’s opened up about his own experiences with feeling stuck in place and missing out on life.

“It was a song that spoke to my personal experience of feeling like I couldn’t move on from certain things,” Wallen told Billboard. “There are times where I feel like I’m stuck in time.”

This personal connection to the song is evident in Wallen’s passionate performances, both on the recorded track and in live shows. The song has become a staple of his setlist, and fans sing along with every word.

The Future of Morgan Wallen

As “7 Summers” continues to dominate the country music charts and permeate the cultural zeitgeist, all eyes are on Morgan Wallen to see what’s next. With his soulful voice, relatable lyrics, and undeniable charm, he’s quickly becoming a household name in the music industry.

But no matter what the future holds, “7 Summers” will remain a touchstone for Wallen and his fans. It’s a testament to the power of music and the timeless themes that connect us all.
