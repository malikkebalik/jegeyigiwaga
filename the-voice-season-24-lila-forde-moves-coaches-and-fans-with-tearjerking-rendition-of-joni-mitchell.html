Who is 'The Voice' Season 24's Lila Forde?

Forde's artistic career was formed by her mother, a brilliant concert pianist known for her knowledge of classical music and crystal bowls.

Her parents, raised in a spiritual household in Seattle, have spent the last 20 years as meditation teachers and the last five as proud proprietors of a meditation camp resort tucked in the heart of the Pacific Northwest.

Forde's parents arrange immersive experiences for their visitors, such as sound baths and meditation retreats. Owing to her musical prowess, 'The Voice' contestant studied jazz voice at the USC Thornton School of Music, where she fell in love with songwriting.

Last fall, Forde embarked on her first tour, touring Europe as the opening act for singer-songwriter Rachel Mazer. She now sings at posh hotels, farmers markets, and private gatherings and is presently working on her debut album, which she was able to create thanks to a $16,000 Kickstarter campaign.
