Discovering Kayla Burton's Journey: A Journalism Maven with a Strong Sports Legacy

August 2024 · 3 minute read

Kayla Burton, a dynamic 28-year-old journalist, has carved an exceptional career in the realms of sports and journalism, serving as an inspiration to many. With a robust foundation in journalism, she has swiftly ascended to prominence, currently holding the position of a host and reporter at ESPN. This piece delves into the remarkable career and life of Kayla Burton, a name poised to make waves in the future of journalism.

Kayla Burton’s professional journey spans media and sports, with roles including head basketball coach at the Rivers School and a sports reporter for the Celtics Late Night Show. Following this, she moved to Syracuse, New York, to work as a sports reporter for the ACC Network, covering a diverse range of sports such as Syracuse football, basketball, lacrosse, and field hockey.

August 2019 saw Kayla stepping into the role of a reporter and content producer for WGGB, ABC40|FOX6 in Springfield, Massachusetts. In addition to leading the Friday Fever player profile pieces and the reporting team, she also operates as a multimedia journalist, delivering live reports from 5 a.m. to 9 p.m. Furthermore, in October 2021, Kayla will transition to NFL Media, taking on the role of a reporter and content producer. Embracing her passion for sports and storytelling, she thrives on meeting new individuals and sharing their stories with the world.

Kayla Burton Wiki: Age, Height, Weight, and More

Kayla Burton was born on June 15, 1995, standing at 5’8″ and weighing 56 kg. Her journey commenced as a student-athlete at Lehigh University, where she pursued basketball and obtained a bachelor’s degree in journalism. Adding to her academic accomplishments, Kayla holds a master’s degree in broadcast journalism from the Newhouse School of Public Communications. As of 2024, she is single and fully devoted to her career, rapidly establishing herself as a standout figure in the field of journalism.

Sports And Media Families

Kayla takes pride in her family’s rich background in sports and media. Steve Burton, her father, serves as a long-time television personality and sports director for WBZ-TV. Her uncle, Paul Burton, is a general assignment reporter for WBZ-TV News, while her grandfather, Ron Burton, was the first conscripted Boston patriot. Additionally, her siblings, excelling in basketball and football across different institutions, further contribute to their family’s athletic legacy.

Notably, her elder sister, Kendall Burton, pursued basketball at Villanova University and graduated with a degree in communications. Meanwhile, her younger brother, Austin Burton, showcases his talent as a quarterback for the UCLA football team. As for her youngest sister, Veronica Burton, she shines as a guard with the Dallas Wings in the WNBA, garnering acclaim for her on-court prowess, particularly as an effective defender. In her rookie season, she recorded an average of 7.6 points, 3.9 rebounds, 2.9 assists, and 1.8 steals per game, contributing to the family’s pride in their sports heritage.

Kayla Burton’s Boston Marathon Feat

Kayla Burton partook in the 125th Boston Marathon, held on October 11, 2021, which marked the first instance of the marathon being hosted in October due to the COVID-19 pandemic. She completed the 26.2-mile distance between Hopkinton and Boston in 4 hours, 29 minutes, and 33 seconds, securing the 12th overall position and ranking 5th among women. In a heartwarming father-daughter moment, her father, Steve Burton, presented her with the medal live on TV.
