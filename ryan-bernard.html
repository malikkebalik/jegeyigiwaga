Ryan Bernard’s Funeral Home, and Burial By The Bernards

R. Bernard Funeral Services was founded by Ryan. He founded the company in 2017 and hopes that his family will carry on his legacy and pass it down to future generations. He is particularly confident that his daughters will inherit the family business from him.

Ryan converted an old bank building into a funeral and cremation home, keeping the “old traditional funeral stuff” but also adding new things. Ryan once stated, “I am 41 years old.” I’m not just trying to reach out to grandmothers and grandfathers; I’m also attempting to reach out to millennials and baby boomers.”

Concerning the television show Buried By The Bernards, Ryan and his family were cast on Netflix after their television commercial went viral in 2018. The Bernards first appeared on the Steve Harvey Show in 2017 for their headline-making drive-thru window viewings. The commercial depicted a dead body rising from a coffin to lament his own funeral expenses.

Events like this piqued the interest of a producer, and thus the show was born.

Ryan previously stated to WATN, a Memphis-based television station, that he and his family did not want to be a part of it at first. However, they changed their minds when the producers announced, “Hey, we’re going to make this into a comedy — your uncle Kevin and your mom.” We’re going to make a comedy out of this, and we’re going to focus on the family rather than the business.”

Ryan responded, “We thought that was a great idea, so we just went with it because this is what we do every day.”

You can now see for yourself how the Bernards have been assisting families in their grief while running their low-cost funeral home in the series, which was shot between 2019 and 2020, before the covid-19 pandemic.
